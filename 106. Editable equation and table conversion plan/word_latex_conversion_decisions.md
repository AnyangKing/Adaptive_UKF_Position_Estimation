# Word/LaTeX conversion decisions

## Recommended route

Use Word for professor review and final local inspection; keep LaTeX-style math source as the equation reference.

Reason:

- the professor likely reviews Word more easily;
- equations can still be entered as editable Word equations from the LaTeX source;
- figures can be visually checked directly in Word;
- table width problems are easier to see early.

## Do not do yet

- Do not finalize a Sensors template without human target-journal confirmation.
- Do not invent author/funding/conflict metadata.
- Do not remove negative results to make tables shorter.
- Do not rasterize equations.

## Conversion artifact naming recommendation

When actual conversion starts, use:

- `107. Word template dry conversion`

or, if staying template-neutral:

- `107. Editable manuscript conversion dry run`

## Minimum acceptance criteria for actual conversion

- all equations editable;
- Table 1 and Table 2 readable at intended page width;
- Fig.1--6 inserted or referenced in numerical order;
- back matter placeholders visible and not final-looking;
- references converted or explicitly marked style-pending;
- no local-only handoff/study/professor files included.
