# Risk register while proceeding without professor feedback

## R1. Wrong target journal

Risk:

- Time may be spent formatting for a journal the professor later rejects.

Mitigation:

- Keep the next manuscript package journal-neutral.
- Do not convert into a final template until target is selected.

## R2. Simulation-only weakness

Risk:

- Some journals may expect field or laboratory validation.

Mitigation:

- Frame current work as reproducible signal-level validation.
- Prepare a real-water validation plan as a future fork.
- Avoid claiming deployment readiness.

## R3. Novelty overclaim

Risk:

- Reviewers may cite prior radar frequency agility, frequency-hopped USBL, or Costas USBL.

Mitigation:

- Keep the 96번 references and Table 1 positioning.
- Claim mechanism/boundary, not first use of frequency diversity.

## R4. Moving-target confusion

Risk:

- Reader may think the paper promises moving-target localization improvement.

Mitigation:

- Keep moving results in the boundary section.
- State explicitly that moving RMSE gain is not validated.

## R5. Excessively long method section

Risk:

- v3/v4 method detail may be too long for some journals.

Mitigation:

- Keep a full reproducibility version.
- Prepare a compressed main-text version plus supplement-ready details.

## R6. Professor later changes direction

Risk:

- Professor may prefer real-water validation or a different novelty angle.

Mitigation:

- Make all current work modular.
- Keep numbered folders documenting decisions.
- Preserve conservative claims so later redirection is not destructive.
