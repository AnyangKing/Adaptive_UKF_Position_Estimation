# Journal shortlist and fit

This is a working shortlist for independent progress while the professor is away. It should be revisited after professor feedback.

## Sources checked

- Applied Acoustics official ScienceDirect aims/scope page: underwater acoustics and acoustic signal processing are explicitly in scope; the page also says papers should include an experimental component or experimental validation of a proposed theoretical or numerical model.
- Sensors official MDPI aims/scope page: sensor science and applications, reproducibility/detail, signal processing and data fusion in sensor systems, and multi-sensor positioning/navigation are explicitly in scope.

## Option A: Sensors

Working fit:

- Strong fit for sensor fusion, reproducibility, signal processing, and multi-sensor positioning/navigation.
- The current manuscript is code-audited and has detailed simulation protocol, tables, and figure package.
- The TOA/TDOA/DOA-UKF framing aligns with sensor data fusion.

Main risk:

- Need to make the paper look like a sensor-system / measurement-design contribution, not only an acoustics simulation.

Recommended framing:

> Carrier-agile observation design for compact USBL sensor arrays, evaluated in a reproducible TOA/TDOA/DOA-UKF localization pipeline.

Current suitability:

- High as the default self-directed target.

## Option B: Applied Acoustics

Working fit:

- Strong topical fit for underwater acoustics and acoustic signal processing.
- The mechanism is acoustic: gated surface reflection, coherent interference, carrier-sensitive DOA bias.

Main risk:

- The official scope emphasizes experimental component or experimental validation of theoretical/numerical models. Our current work is simulation/signal-level validation, not lake/sea experimental validation.

Recommended framing if chosen:

> Applied underwater acoustic localization method with numerical validation and clear practical USBL scenario.

Current suitability:

- Medium/high, but likely stronger after either real-water validation or a carefully written numerical-model validation argument.

## Option C: IEEE/OES-style oceanic engineering venue

Working fit:

- Strong topic fit for USBL, underwater navigation, and ocean engineering.

Main risk:

- Likely higher expectation for field validation, hardware relevance, or ocean-engineering deployment maturity.
- Official scope/details should be manually checked through IEEE/OES and recent accepted papers before committing.

Current suitability:

- Ambitious target or post-real-water target.

## Working recommendation

Unless the professor later redirects:

1. Prepare a clean Sensors-style manuscript package first.
2. Keep Applied Acoustics as an alternative if the acoustic mechanism is emphasized and the numerical validation is accepted as sufficient.
3. Treat IEEE/OES-style submission as the stretch target, especially if real-water validation becomes feasible.

## Official-source notes

Applied Acoustics:

- Includes underwater acoustics and acoustic signal processing.
- Requires experimental component or experimental validation of a proposed theoretical/numerical model.

Sensors:

- Covers sensor science/technology and applications.
- Explicitly includes signal processing and data fusion in sensor systems and multi-sensor positioning/navigation.
- Encourages detailed experimental/theoretical reporting for reproducibility.
