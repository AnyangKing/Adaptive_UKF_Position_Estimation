# Claim boundary check

The package must preserve the manuscript's conservative claim boundary.

## Keep

- Static 600 m is the main positive performance result.
- Frequency agility is framed as carrier-agile whitening of post-gating coherent multipath DOA bias.
- TOA/TDOA/DOA adaptive-R UKF is the fixed receiver-side tracking backbone.
- Moving-target experiments support residual whitening and boundary analysis, not reliable RMSE improvement.
- Quasi-static continuous boundary remains 0.005 m/s.
- Compact aperture explains why sub-meter 600 m performance is not expected.

## Avoid

- "First frequency-hopping USBL" claim.
- "Moving-target RMSE improvement" claim.
- "Validated to 0.1 m/s" quasi-static claim.
- "Sub-meter long-range accuracy" claim.
- Any implication that carrier agility removes all multipath or aperture limitations.
